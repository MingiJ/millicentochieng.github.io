About me

I am a final year student taking MSc. Data Science at the African Center of Excellence in Data Science in the University of Rwanda. I have an immense interest in Computer Vision; deep learning models in image recognition, Natural Language Processing and Probabilistic Modeling. My goal at the present time, is to employ necessary machine learning approaches to solve real-world problems.

Currently, I am a research intern at the Harvard University. My research is focused on Transfer learning for classification of bean plant leaf diseases in precision agriculture, towards improving the quality and quantity of agricultural products in East Africa. I am advised by Dr. Weiwei Pan, Research Associate at Harvard University and Dr. Melanie Fernandez Pradier, Senior Researcher at Microsoft Research.

Before joining University of Rwanda, I graduated with a First Class Honors degree in BSc. Applied Statistics with Computing from Moi University, Kenya.
