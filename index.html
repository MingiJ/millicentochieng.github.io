About me

I am a Data and Applied Research Scientist at Microsoft Africa Research Institute, working on Artificial Intelligence research to improve productivity in three broad areas: Work, Health and Society. I have an immense interest in Computer Vision, Natural Language Processing, Sequential Transfer Learning, Probabilistic Modeling and Interpretable Machine Learning. My goal at the present time, is to apply necessary state-of-the-art machine learning techniques to solve impactful real-life problems.

I am also a final year student taking MSc. Data Science at the African Center of Excellence in Data Science in University of Rwanda. Currently, I am conducting my thesis research focused on transfer learning for classification of bean plant leaf diseases in precision agriculture; towards improving the quality and quantity of agricultural products in East Africa. I am advised by Prof. Weiwei Pan, Research Associate at Harvard University and Dr. Melanie Fernandez Pradier, Senior Researcher at Microsoft Research.

Previously, I was a research intern at Harvard University and before joining University of Rwanda, I graduated with a First Class Honors degree in BSc. Applied Statistics with Computing from Moi University, Kenya.
